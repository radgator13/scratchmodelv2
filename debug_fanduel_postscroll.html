Popular Ways to Bet on MLB

When betting on MLB, you'll come across several popular bet types that cater to different preferences and strategies. Here's a quick rundown of the most common ways to bet on Major League Baseball at FanDuel Sportsbook:


  • MLB Moneylines

    This is a straightforward bet on which team will win the game outright, with no point adjustments.

  • MLB Spreads

    Here, you're betting on the outcome of the game with a point spread, which levels the odds between the underdog and the favorite. The spread represents the number of runs by which the favorite is expected to win, while the underdog can either win the game or lose by fewer runs than the spread for the bet to be successful.

  • MLB Totals

    Also known as over/under bets, this wager involves predicting the total number of runs scored by both teams in a game.

  • Live MLB Odds

    In-play betting allows you to place bets on MLB games as they happen. Point spreads and moneylines will update throughout the game to reflect the current state of play.

  • MLB Prop Bets

    These bets focus on the individual or team performance within a game, rather than the final outcome. Examples include betting on a player's number of strikeouts or home runs, or the number of runs scored in a specific inning. You can find prop bets in the "More Wagers" section of our odds tables.

  • MLB Parlay Bets

    Parlays involve combining two or more baseball bets into a single wager, increasing the potential payout. Keep in mind that all bets within the parlay must win for the bet to payout.

  • Partial Game Odds

    This type of bet focuses on a specific portion of the game, such as which team will be leading or score more runs in a certain number of innings. Popular options include 1st Half lines or leader after a specific number of innings.

How to Read MLB Betting Odds

As with any online sports betting odds, you will see either a plus sign (+) or a minus sign (-) next to the MLB lines to indicate the likelihood of that team winning. When you read MLB odds, any odds with a plus sign means you will earn that much money for every $100 you wager. Any baseball odds with the minus sign means you need to bet that much money in order to win $100.

As an example, if you wanted to bet on an MLB game and placed a $150 outright bet on the New York Yankees to win at -150 odds, that bet would pay $250 (your original $150 stake and $100 in winnings). If you wagered $100 on the Tampa Bay Rays to win at +200 odds, you would earn $300 (your original $100 bet and $200 in winnings).



MLB Odds FAQs and Baseball Betting Resources


  • What is the difference between Listed Pitcher Option and Action?

    When betting on moneylines, selecting Action is betting a MLB game regardless of the starting pitchers. One Specified Pitcher or Both Specified Pitchers means the bet will be voided and refunded if the given pitchers get scratched pregame and do not start. All other game markets are considered Action and wagers will remain open regardless of any personnel change. Wagers will be settled at the odds at bet placement.

  • What happens to my bet if a baseball game is suspended, delayed, or shortened?

    Results in an MLB game are official after 5 innings (or 4.5 innings if the home team is winning). In case of a shortened game (most likely due to weather), all wagers which have already been unequivocally determined will stand. For example, if 7 runs have already been scored and the total was set at 6.5.

    All wagers will stand if a game is suspended and finishes the following day. All wagers also stand in suspended MLB playoff games.
